CHOW MEIN RECIPE

INGREDIENTS

  1. 8 cups water, (1.9L)
  2. 6 ounces dried chow mein noodles, (170g)
  3. 3 tablespoons oyster sauce, (45ml)
  4. 1 tablespoon soy sauce , (15ml)
  5. 1 teaspoon sesame oil, (5ml)
  6. 1/4 cup unsalted vegetable broth, (60ml)
  7. 1 teaspoon cornstarch, (3g)
  8. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, (30ml)
  9. 1 teaspoon minced garlic, (3g)
  10. 1/2 teaspoon minced ginger, (3g)
  11. 1 cup green cabbage, (60g) 1/4 inch thick slices
  12. 1/2 cup carrots, (40g) shredded
  13. 1/2 cup bean sprouts, (30g)
  14. 1/4 cup green onions, (10g) 1 1/2 inch long pieces

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a large pot bring 8 cups of water to a boil.
  2. Add dried noodles and cook according to manufacturers directions, until tender with some chew.
  3. Drain noodles into a colander and rinse with cold water to stop cooking process.
  4. Drain well and set aside.
  5. In a small bowl whisk together oyster sauce, soy sauce, sesame oil.
  6. Then add vegetable broth, and cornstarch for chow mein sauce. Set aside.
  7. Heat a large wok or saute pan over medium-high heat. Once hot add the vegetable oil.
  8. Once the oil is very hot and just begins to smoke, add in garlic and ginger.
  9. Stir-fry for 20 seconds being careful not to burn the aromatics.
  10. Add the cabbage and carrots, stir-fry until just tender, 2 minutes.
  11. Add the noodles and chow mein sauce.
  12. Toss and stir to combine, cook until sauce thickens and coats noodles, 1 minute.
  13. Add bean sprouts and green onions, stir-fry until just tender, 1 minute.
