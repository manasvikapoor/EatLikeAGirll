RAMEN RECIPE

INGREDIENTS

  1. 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  2. 3 cloves garlic, minced
  3. 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
  4. 1/2 cup rice vinegar, or to taste
  5. 1/2 cup reduced-sodium soy sauce
  6. 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  7. 2 tablespoons mirin
  8. 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  9. 1 tablespoon sriracha sauce
  10. 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  11. 1 cup water
  12. 1 cooked chicken breasts, shredded
  13. 3/4 pound baby bok choy, quartered lengthwise
  14. 3 (3 ounce) packages dried ramen noodles
  15. 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  16. 4 eggs
  17. 1 jalapeno pepper, sliced, or to taste
  18. 2 green onions, chopped, or to taste
  19. 1/4 cup chopped cilantro, or to taste
    20. /

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Heat a Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  2. Add onion and saute until starting to soften, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add garlic and ginger; cook about 30 seconds more.
  4. Add rice vinegar, soy sauce, oyster sauce, mirin, fish sauce, and sriracha sauce; stir to combine.
  5. Add chicken broth and water and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat and simmer about 5 minutes.
  7. Add chicken and bok choy; simmer gently until bok choy is just tender, about 2 minutes.
  8. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil.
  9. Add ramen noodles and return to a boil.
  10. Cook, uncovered, stirring occasionally, until noodles are tender yet firm to the bite, 5 to 7 minutes.
  11. Drain and drizzle with vegetable oil to prevent sticking.
  12. While ramen is cooking, fill a pot with enough water to cover the eggs and bring to a boil.
  13. Gently lower eggs into the boiling water and cook until yolks are barely set, about 7 minutes.
  14. Fill a large bowl with ice water.
  15. Transfer soft boiled eggs to the ice bath to stop the cooking process; let sit about 3 minutes.
  16. Drain eggs, carefully peel away shells, and slice in half.
  17. Divide ramen between 4 bowls. Add broth.
  18. Top each with jalapeno, green onions, cilantro, and soft boiled egg. Serve immediately.
