1.) COQ AU VIN

Coq au vin ("rooster/cock with wine") is a French dish of chicken braised with wine, lardons, mushrooms, and optionally garlic. A red Burgundy wine is typically used,[2] though many regions of France make variants using local wines, such as coq au vin jaune (Jura), coq au Riesling (Alsace), coq au pourpre or coq au violet (Beaujolais nouveau), coq au Champagne, etc.

5 ultimate places for Coq au Vin in Paris are:

1. La Jacobin, 59-61 Rue Saint-André des Arts

2. A la Biche au Bois, 45 Avenue Ledru-Rollin

3. Auberge Bressane, 16 Avenue de la Motte-Picquet

4. Le Coq Rico, 98 rue Lepic

5. La Cuisine de Philippe, 25 rue Servandoni

2.) FRENCH TOAST

French toast is a dish made of sliced bread soaked in eggs and milk, then fried. Alternative names and variants include eggy bread, Bombay toast, German toast, gypsy toast, poor knights (of Windsor), torrija, preggled challah and Arme Riddere.Slices of bread are soaked or dipped in a mixture of beaten eggs, often whisked with milk or cream. Sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and vanilla may be variously added to the mixture. The bread is then fried in butter or olive oil until browned and cooked through. Day-old bread is often used, both for its thrift and because it will soak up more egg mixture without falling apart. The cooked slices may be served with sugar or sweet toppings such as jam, honey, fruit or maple syrup.

5 ultimate places for French Toast in Paris are:

1. Le Matignon, 3 avenue Matignon

2. Ladurée, 75 Avenue des Champs Elysées (Rue Lincoln)

3. Bistrotters, 9 rue Decrès

4. Hardware Société, 10 rue Lamarck

5. Hôtel Plaza Athénée, 25 avenue Montaigne (Rue Clément Marot)
